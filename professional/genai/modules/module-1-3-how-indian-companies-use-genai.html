How Indian Companies Use GenAI

⏱ 12 min read 📝 Assignment inside

You now understand what GenAI is and which tools to use. This module answers the question that matters most for your career: what is actually happening inside the companies you want to work at?

TCS, Infosys, Wipro, and HCL are not experimenting with AI. They are deploying it in production — right now, at scale — across their client projects and internal operations. Understanding what they are building, and which roles are involved, gives you a concrete advantage in every interview and placement drive you attend from here on.

TCS — Agentic AI at Enterprise Scale

  • Agentic AI for financial services and supply chain — AI agents that orchestrate actual business transactions inside client value chains using reasoning and planning capabilities of LLMs

  • 150+ specialised agentic AI solutions across financial services, accounting, and supply chain management — deployed for global enterprise clients

  • AI-powered AWS integrations — modernising legacy enterprise systems using GenAI for faster triage, code generation, and testing

  • NVIDIA partnership for AI products in telecom and autonomous systems — TCS is building AI-embedded products, not just services

  • University of Cincinnati partnership — "My First AI Job" programme to prepare entry-level talent for AI careers (directly relevant to you)

TCS describes GenAI as enabling "agentic AI to orchestrate actual transactions inside business value chains." In your interview, show you understand the difference between a chatbot (answering questions) and an AI agent (taking actions). Mentioning agentic AI and how it applies to the domain you are interviewing for will make you stand out immediately.

Infosys — Topaz Platform and AI at Client Delivery Scale

  • Infosys Topaz — an AI-first services platform combining GenAI technologies across software development, HR, recruitment, sales, vendor management, and industry solutions

  • Automated SDLC documentation and code generation — partnered with AWS Amazon Q Developer to automate software development lifecycle work including code generation, debugging, testing, and legacy modernisation

  • 100+ new GenAI agents being developed for client deployment — automating productivity, operations, and growth tasks across 200 major clients

  • Cambridge AI lab — collaboration with the University of Cambridge focused on advanced AI research

  • Manufacturing, telecom, financial services, and consumer goods — the four priority verticals where Infosys is deploying GenAI solutions at scale in 2026

Infosys uses the term "Infosys Topaz" constantly in their communications. Look it up before your interview — understanding what it is and how it differs from a generic AI tool shows you follow industry news. Also, 28 million lines of AI-generated code means developers at Infosys are already using AI coding tools daily. Mentioning your experience with GitHub Copilot or Claude for code generation is highly relevant.

Wipro — Consulting-Led, AI-Powered Operating Model

  • AI-powered operating model launched February 2026 — combines advisory, AI, and enterprise transformation into one end-to-end service — connecting boardroom strategy to scaled operations

  • Intelligent Agents for financial data analysis — deployed for a UK-based financial services group to analyse payments data, generate intelligence dashboards, fraud detection alerts, and compliance reports in real time

  • AI-first cybersecurity operations — deployed for a leading apparel company to streamline IT operations, improve regulatory compliance, and automate security workflows

  • Prior authorisation workflows in healthcare — using AI to reduce turnaround times for a US-based healthcare payer's member services

  • Developer productivity via GenAI — 60+ GenAI use cases for software testing optimisation across client projects

Wipro's new operating model emphasises "persona-based, end-to-end enterprise transformation." In practice, this means they want people who can connect an AI tool to a specific business outcome — not just people who know how to use the tool. Frame your AI knowledge in terms of business impact: "I used AI to reduce X time" or "I used AI to improve Y output" rather than "I know how to use ChatGPT."
